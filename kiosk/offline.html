1
Get an Ethernet Cable
Find a standard RJ45 Ethernet cable. These are usually available near your WiFi router or network switch.
2
Connect to the Raspberry Pi
Plug one end of the Ethernet cable into the Ethernet port on the side of the Raspberry Pi. You'll feel a click when it's properly seated.
3
Connect to Router or Network Switch
Plug the other end into any available LAN port on your WiFi router or network switch. The port should light up.
4
Wait for Auto-Connection
The Raspberry Pi will automatically detect and configure the Ethernet connection. This screen will close automatically once internet is detected.
💡
Troubleshooting
If the connection doesn't work after 30 seconds, try unplugging and replugging the cable. Make sure the LEDs on both the Pi and router port are blinking.
